1. Admin Overview

The Royale Jazz admin dashboard is the hotel’s internal workspace for operations and management. It is used to control branding, manage reservations, assign rooms, track guest history, maintain room inventory, and manage staff access.

Important: Different users see different pages depending on their role. Owners have the broadest access. Managers and Front Desk staff see only the areas they need for their daily work.

2. Login Experience

The login screen is branded from the company settings. If no company logo has been added yet, no logo will display. Owners are taken to the Company page after login, while Managers and Front Desk users are taken to the Dashboard.

3. User Roles and Permissions

There are three user roles in the system: Owner, Manager, and Front Desk.

Owner

Owners can manage company branding, hotels, staff access, reservations, rooms, guests, and property information.

Manager

Managers can run daily hotel operations, including reservations, guests, rooms, and property content, but cannot manage company settings or staff access.

Front Desk

Front Desk users focus on live guest operations: arrivals, departures, reservations, room assignment, and guest history.

4. How to Get a User Into the System

A staff member must already have a login account before access can be granted to a hotel. After that, an Owner can open Staff Access and grant the user the correct role.

  1. Create the staff member’s login account.
  2. Confirm the staff member can sign in.
  3. Log in as an Owner.
  4. Open Staff Access.
  5. Enter the staff email address.
  6. Choose the role: Owner, Manager, or Front Desk.
  7. Save the access record.

5. Company Management

The Company page is owner-only and controls the overall look and feel of the admin dashboard.

Owners can update:

  • brand name
  • admin subtitle
  • logo URL
  • primary color
  • accent color
  • support email
  • support phone

Owners can also add hotels from this page by entering the hotel’s details and saving the new record.

6. Reservations and Booking Workflow

The Reservations page is the main booking management screen. Staff can create, edit, confirm, cancel, check in, check out, assign rooms, and delete reservations from this area.

Reservation lifecycle

  1. Pending: a request exists but has not yet been accepted.
  2. Confirmed: the booking has been accepted and is expected to arrive.
  3. Checked In: the guest is currently in house.
  4. Checked Out: the stay is complete.

A reservation can also be marked Cancelled.

Important: A room should be assigned before check-in. The workflow is designed so that staff can confirm and prepare a reservation first, then assign the room, then complete check-in.

Manual booking steps

  1. Open Reservations.
  2. Click Add Reservation.
  3. Enter the guest’s details.
  4. Select the room type and dates.
  5. Set the nightly rate and total amount.
  6. Assign a room if one is already known.
  7. Save the reservation.

Public booking request steps

  1. Open Reservations.
  2. Filter to Pending.
  3. Review guest details, dates, and room type.
  4. Confirm availability.
  5. Assign a room when appropriate.
  6. Click Confirm.

7. Rooms, Guests, and Properties

Guests

The Guests page gives the team a running history of guest contact details, spend, stay count, active stay information, and notes.

Rooms

The Rooms page manages both room types and physical room inventory. Staff can maintain room status, housekeeping state, occupancy, and media attached to room types.

Properties

The Properties page is available to Owners and Managers and is used for hotel content and configuration management.

8. Daily Operations Checklist

Start of day

  1. Open the Dashboard.
  2. Review arrivals, unassigned rooms, and in-house stays.
  3. Open Reservations and prepare any pending arrivals.

During the day

  1. Process new bookings.
  2. Confirm pending requests.
  3. Assign rooms.
  4. Check guests in.
  5. Update room readiness and housekeeping.

End of day

  1. Review in-house guests.
  2. Check out departures.
  3. Update room statuses.
  4. Leave notes for the next shift.

9. Quick Reference

Owners manage the company, hotels, staff access, and all hotel operations.

Managers manage reservations, guests, rooms, and property content.

Front Desk manages day-to-day booking activity, guest arrivals, room assignment, and departures.
